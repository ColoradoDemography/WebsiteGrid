State Demography Summit
The Colorado State Demography Summit is held in the Fall of each year. This one-day conference offers attendees the opportunity to learn about the most current population and economic conditions and forecasts for the state and its regions and what is driving these changes.
Registration details are announced in SDO's Monthly Newsletter.

The 2022 State Demography Summit was held virtually on Friday, November 4, from 8:45 a.m.to 4:00 p.m.

This one-day conference offers attendees the opportunity to learn about the most current population and economic estimates and forecasts for the state and its regions. The morning sessions will cover the economic and population conditions and outlook for the state and their implications. The afternoon session will dig deeper into data tools, data reliability, and data definitions.

  • Plenary sessions will cover the most current population estimates and forecasts, migration trends, and discuss implications for housing, planning, policy, business, and public services.
  • Expert economists from across the state will discuss the near-term outlook for Colorado’s economy and thoughts about the state’s long run competitiveness.
  • State Demography Office staff will present tools for visualizing population change and will introduce its redesigned website.
  • An expert from the U.S. Census Bureau will discuss new Census data products including their data equity tools.
  • Additional sessions will cover data reliability issues with Census products as well as a discussion and tools surrounding the concept of “Disadvantaged Communities” a historically common reference now in the limelight due to COVID relief dollars.
